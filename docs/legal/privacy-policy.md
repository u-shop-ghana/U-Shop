## 2. What Personal Data We Collect

We collect personal data in the following categories:

### 2.1 Account and Identity Data

When you create an account on U-Shop, we collect:
- **Full name** as provided during registration
- **Email address** — including institutional email addresses (e.g., `.edu.gh` domains) used for student verification
- **Date of birth** (where provided, for age verification purposes)
- **Profile photograph** (optional, where you choose to upload one)
- **Account type** (Buyer, Student Seller, or Reseller)
- **Verification status** and verification documents (see Section 2.5)

### 2.2 Contact and Delivery Data

For transactions, we collect:
- **Physical delivery address** — including street address, city, region, and Ghana Post GPS code where applicable
- **Phone number** — including Mobile Money (MoMo) numbers required for payment and transaction communication
- **Preferred delivery method** (courier or in-person campus meetup)

### 2.3 Financial and Transaction Data

We collect the following financial data to facilitate transactions:
- **Transaction records** — product purchased or sold, transaction amounts, dates, platform fees, and escrow status
- **Mobile Money (MoMo) account number** — collected from Sellers to facilitate payouts via Paystack
- **Bank account details** — collected from Sellers who elect to receive payouts via bank transfer, processed through Paystack
- **Wallet balance and transaction history** within the Platform

**What we do NOT store:**
- Raw credit or debit card numbers
- Card verification values (CVV/CVC codes)
- Card PINs or online banking credentials

All card payment processing is handled directly and securely by **Paystack** in accordance with Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) requirements. U-Shop does not receive or store your card details. For information about how Paystack handles your payment data, see [Paystack's Privacy Policy](https://paystack.com/gh/privacy).

### 2.4 Listing and Store Data (Sellers)

Where you operate as a Seller, we collect:
- **Store name, handle (URL slug), and storefront description**
- **Product listings** — titles, descriptions, photographs, condition grades, pricing, and stock information
- **Custom return and warranty policy text** as set by the Seller
- **IMEI numbers** for phone listings, where required
- **Serial numbers** for laptop listings above GH₵ 500

### 2.5 Verification Documents

For student verification and Seller identity verification, we may collect:
- **Student identification documents** — student ID cards or university letters confirming enrolment
- **National identification documents** — Ghana Card or passport, where required for Seller verification

These documents are processed solely for verification purposes. Student ID documents are deleted from our systems within 30 days of a verification decision (approval or rejection) in accordance with our data minimisation obligations under the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012. National ID documents used for ongoing Seller compliance are retained for the duration of the Seller's account and for 3 years after account closure, in compliance with applicable financial record-keeping regulations.

### 2.6 Usage and Technical Data

When you use the Platform, we automatically collect:
- **Device information** — device type, operating system, browser type and version
- **IP address** and approximate location derived from IP
- **Pages visited** and features used within the Platform
- **Referral source** — how you arrived at the Platform (e.g., a direct store URL shared on WhatsApp)
- **Session data** — login timestamps, session duration
- **Error logs** — crash reports and error data to improve Platform stability

### 2.7 Communications Data

We retain records of:
- **In-app messages** between Buyers and Sellers within the Platform's messaging system
- **Dispute communications** submitted through our dispute resolution process
- **Support communications** — emails and messages exchanged with our support team
- **Notifications sent** — records of emails, SMS, and in-app notifications we have sent you

Message content within the Platform is retained for 24 months after the relevant transaction is completed or the account is closed, primarily for dispute resolution and fraud prevention purposes.

## 4. Mobile Money (MoMo) Data: A Specific Note

Given the central role of Mobile Money in Ghana's digital payments ecosystem, we want to be particularly clear about how MoMo data is handled.

- **Buyer MoMo numbers:** Where you pay using Mobile Money, your MoMo number is passed to Paystack for payment processing. We do not store your MoMo number for payment purposes beyond what is required to reconcile the transaction in our records.
- **Seller MoMo numbers:** Seller payout MoMo numbers are collected and stored within our platform to facilitate recurring payouts via Paystack. This data is encrypted at rest and transmitted over TLS. Sellers may update their payout MoMo number at any time from their account settings.
- **We do not share MoMo numbers** with third parties other than Paystack for the purpose of processing payments and payouts.

## 7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active and for a defined period after account closure, as follows:

| Data Category | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Account data (name, email) | Duration of account + 3 years after closure |
| Transaction records and escrow history | 6 years after transaction date (financial record-keeping) |
| Student ID documents | Deleted within 30 days of verification decision |
| National ID documents (Seller verification) | Duration of account + 3 years after closure |
| In-app messages and dispute records | 24 months after transaction completion or account closure |
| Usage and analytics data | 13 months from collection (standard analytics cycle) |
| Marketing consent records | Until consent is withdrawn + 3 years |

After these periods expire, data is deleted or irreversibly anonymised, except where longer retention is required by applicable law.

## 8. Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational security measures to protect your personal data, including:
- Encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Encryption of sensitive data at rest (identification documents, payout account details)
- Access controls limiting staff access to personal data on a need-to-know basis
- Regular security assessments of our infrastructure and third-party processors
- Multi-factor authentication for Platform administrators

No security system is completely impenetrable. In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected users and the Data Protection Commission as required under the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 and our Data Protection Policy.

## 9. Your Rights Under the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012

As a data subject under the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843), you have the following rights:

| Right | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Right of Access** | You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you |
| **Right to Correction** | You may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data |
| **Right to Deletion** | You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to our legal retention obligations |
| **Right to Restriction** | You may request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances |
| **Right to Object** | You may object to processing based on our legitimate interests |
| **Right to Withdraw Consent** | Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time |
| **Right to Complain** | You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Commission of Ghana |

**How to exercise your rights:**
Submit your request in writing to **privacy@u-shop.com** with the subject line "Data Subject Request." Include your full name, registered email address, and a description of your request. We will respond within **21 days** of receiving a valid request. We may request proof of identity before processing your request.

**Data Protection Commission of Ghana:**
If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Commission of Ghana at [www.dataprotection.org.gh](http://www.dataprotection.org.gh).
