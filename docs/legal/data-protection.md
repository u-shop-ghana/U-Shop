## 2. Data Protection Principles — Our Obligations Under Act 843

The Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012 establishes foundational principles that govern all personal data processing. U-Shop Ghana Limited is bound by and committed to upholding these principles in every aspect of our data operations.

### 2.1 Principle 1: Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency

We will only process personal data:
- With the **consent** of the data subject, where required
- Where processing is **necessary for the performance of a contract** with the data subject (e.g., processing a transaction)
- Where processing is necessary for **compliance with a legal obligation** (e.g., financial record-keeping requirements)
- Where processing is necessary for our **legitimate interests**, provided those interests are not overridden by the data subject's rights and interests

We maintain a Record of Processing Activities (ROPA) document that maps every data processing activity to its legal basis.

**Transparency:** We will always inform data subjects about what data we collect, why we collect it, and what we do with it, through our Privacy Policy and at the point of collection where required.

### 2.2 Principle 2: Purpose Limitation

Personal data collected for one purpose will not be used for a different, incompatible purpose without the data subject's knowledge and, where required, consent. Data collected to process a transaction will not be repurposed for unsolicited marketing without separate consent.

### 2.3 Principle 3: Data Minimisation

We collect only the minimum personal data necessary to fulfil the stated purpose. We do not collect data "in case it is useful later." Every data field in our collection systems is justified by a specific operational need.

**Application in our context:**
- We do not collect card numbers or CVV codes — payment card data is processed exclusively by Paystack
- Student ID documents are deleted within 30 days of the verification decision — we retain the verification outcome, not the document
- We do not collect geolocation data beyond what is required for delivery address purposes

### 2.4 Principle 4: Accuracy

We will take reasonable steps to ensure that personal data we hold is accurate and kept up to date. Data subjects may correct inaccurate data through their account settings or by contacting **privacy@u-shop.com**. We will process correction requests within **21 days**.

### 2.5 Principle 5: Storage Limitation

Personal data will not be retained for longer than is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, unless retention is required by law. Our data retention schedule is documented in Section 7 of our Privacy Policy and audited annually.

### 2.6 Principle 6: Integrity and Confidentiality (Security)

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, destruction, or disclosure. These measures are detailed in Section 4 of this Policy.

### 2.7 Principle 7: Accountability

U-Shop Ghana Limited is accountable for compliance with these principles. We maintain documentation of our data processing activities, conduct regular internal reviews of our data practices, and will cooperate fully with the Data Protection Commission in any investigation or audit.

## 3. Rights of Data Subjects Under Act 843

Every individual whose personal data we process has the following rights under the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012. We are committed to upholding these rights fully and without unnecessary delay.

### 3.1 Right of Access

**What it is:** You have the right to request confirmation of whether we hold personal data about you and, if so, to receive a copy of that data, along with information about how it is being used.

**How to exercise it:** Submit a Subject Access Request (SAR) to **privacy@u-shop.com** with the subject line "Subject Access Request." Include your full name, registered email address, and a description of the data you are requesting.

**Our response commitment:** We will respond within **21 days** of receiving a valid, verified request. We may request proof of identity before processing your request to protect against unauthorised disclosure.

**Fees:** We will not charge a fee for a first Subject Access Request in any 12-month period. Where a Buyer requests multiple copies of the same data within 12 months, we may charge a reasonable administrative fee.

### 3.2 Right to Correction

**What it is:** You have the right to request that inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you is corrected without undue delay.

**How to exercise it:** Submit a correction request to **privacy@u-shop.com** or update your details directly in your account settings where the option is available.

**Our response commitment:** We will action corrections within **21 days** and will notify any third-party processors who hold the same data of the correction where practicable.

### 3.3 Right to Deletion ("Right to Be Forgotten")

**What it is:** You have the right to request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances, including:
- The data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected
- You withdraw consent on which processing was based and there is no other legal basis
- You object to processing based on legitimate interests and there are no overriding grounds
- The data has been unlawfully processed

**Limitations:** We may be required by law to retain certain data beyond your deletion request. In particular:
- Financial transaction records must be retained for a minimum period required under Ghanaian financial regulations (typically 6 years)
- Records relevant to an ongoing legal dispute or investigation cannot be deleted until the matter is resolved
- Records required for fraud prevention that relate to a terminated or banned account may be retained for as long as necessary to prevent re-registration

Where we are required to retain data despite a deletion request, we will inform you of the reason and the expected retention period.

**How to exercise it:** Submit a deletion request to **privacy@u-shop.com** with the subject line "Data Deletion Request."

**Our response commitment:** We will respond within **21 days** with confirmation of the data deleted, or an explanation of why deletion is not possible for certain data categories.

### 3.4 Right to Object to Processing

**What it is:** Where we process your data based on our legitimate interests, you have the right to object. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.

You have an absolute right to object to processing for **direct marketing purposes** at any time. Where you object to marketing, we will cease all marketing communications immediately.

**How to exercise it:** Contact **privacy@u-shop.com** with the subject line "Objection to Processing" or use the unsubscribe link in any marketing email.

### 3.5 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we process your data based on consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

**How to exercise it:** Contact **privacy@u-shop.com** or use the consent management features in your account settings.

### 3.6 Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you are not satisfied with our response to a data subject request, or if you believe we are not processing your data in accordance with the Ghana Data Protection Act, 2012, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the **Data Protection Commission of Ghana:**

## 4. Technical and Organisational Security Measures

### 4.1 Technical Measures

U-Shop Ghana Limited implements the following technical security measures to protect personal data:

**Encryption:**
- All data transmitted between users' devices and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Personal data stored in our database infrastructure (hosted on Supabase / [Infrastructure Provider]) is encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption
- Sensitive data fields — including identification document file paths, payout account details, and verification data — are subject to additional application-level encryption

**Access Controls:**
- Access to personal data is restricted to U-Shop personnel on a strict need-to-know basis
- Administrative access to database systems requires multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- User account authentication supports and encourages MFA via email OTP
- All administrative access events are logged and auditable

**Infrastructure Security:**
- Our Platform is hosted on reputable cloud infrastructure with SOC 2 compliance
- Security patches are applied to our infrastructure within 72 hours of release for critical vulnerabilities
- Regular penetration testing is conducted [quarterly / annually — specify] by [internal team / external provider]
- Our payment processing partner, Paystack, operates under PCI DSS compliance, eliminating our exposure to raw payment card data

**Data Isolation:**
- Buyer and Seller data is stored with appropriate access controls to prevent cross-user data exposure
- Row-Level Security (RLS) policies in our database prevent one user from accessing another user's data through API vulnerabilities

## 5. Data Breach Response Procedure

### 5.1 What Constitutes a Data Breach

A personal data breach is any security incident that leads to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed by U-Shop.

### 5.2 Internal Detection and Assessment

**Step 1 — Detection:** Any staff member or contractor who becomes aware of a potential data breach must report it immediately to the designated Data Protection contact (**privacy@u-shop.com**) and to the senior technical team member on duty.

**Step 2 — Containment:** The technical team will take immediate action to contain the breach — isolating affected systems, revoking compromised credentials, and preserving evidence.

**Step 3 — Assessment (within 24 hours):** The breach is assessed to determine:
- What personal data was affected and how many data subjects are involved
- The likely risk to data subjects arising from the breach (low / medium / high / severe)
- Whether the breach is attributable to a third-party processor

### 5.3 Notification to the Data Protection Commission

Where a personal data breach poses a **risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects**, U-Shop Ghana Limited will notify the **Data Protection Commission of Ghana** without undue delay and in any event within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach.

The notification will include:
- A description of the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of data subjects and records affected
- The name and contact details of the Data Protection Officer or data protection contact
- A description of the likely consequences of the breach
- A description of the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the breach, including measures to mitigate its effects

### 5.4 Notification to Affected Data Subjects

Where a breach is likely to result in a **high risk to the rights and freedoms** of affected data subjects, we will notify those individuals directly without undue delay. The notification will:
- Be communicated to affected users via their registered email address and via in-app notification
- Describe in clear, plain language the nature of the breach
- Include the name and contact details of our data protection contact
- Describe the likely consequences of the breach
- Describe the steps we have taken to address the breach
- Advise data subjects on steps they can take to protect themselves (e.g., changing passwords, monitoring MoMo account activity)

### 5.5 Documentation

All data breaches — whether or not they meet the threshold for external notification — will be documented in our internal Data Breach Register, including the facts of the breach, its effects, and the remedial action taken. This register is available for inspection by the Data Protection Commission upon request.
