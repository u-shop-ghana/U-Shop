## 2. Tier 1: Platform Minimum Buyer Protections

### 2.1 What These Protections Are

The following protections apply to **every transaction** on U-Shop and cannot be excluded by any Seller policy:

**Protection 1 — Mandatory Escrow Hold**
All buyer payments are held in escrow for a minimum of 24 hours after the Buyer confirms delivery. No funds are transferred to the Seller before this minimum hold period expires. This protection cannot be waived, not even by agreement between the Buyer and Seller.

**Protection 2 — The Dispute Window**
Every Buyer has the right to open a dispute for any of the qualifying reasons in Section 3 within **48 hours of confirming delivery** through the Platform. This window is guaranteed regardless of the Seller's stated return policy. A Seller's "No Returns" or "All Sales Final" policy does not affect the Buyer's right to open a dispute within this window.

**Protection 3 — Protection Against Fraud and Material Misrepresentation**
Buyers are protected against:
- Receiving an item that is counterfeit or materially different from the item described in the listing
- Receiving an item that is completely non-functional (Dead on Arrival / DOA) with no disclosed defect in the listing
- Receiving a stolen device (confirmed by IMEI or serial number check)
- Not receiving an item at all

For these categories, the Buyer's right to a refund exists regardless of the Seller's policy and regardless of whether the dispute window has technically expired, provided the Buyer reports the issue within a reasonable time and before the escrow funds are disbursed from the Seller's wallet.

**Protection 4 — Automatic Escrow Release Notification**
Before the automatic escrow release window expires (see Section 4), U-Shop will send the Buyer reminder notifications at defined intervals (typically on Day 3, Day 5, Day 6, and 6 hours before auto-release) prompting them to confirm delivery or open a dispute. Failure by U-Shop to send these notifications does not extend the auto-release window, but U-Shop may exercise discretion to accept a late dispute where the notification failure is confirmed.

### 2.2 What "No Returns" Actually Means

A Seller's "No Returns" policy is valid on U-Shop. It means:
- **The Seller does not accept change-of-mind returns.** If a Buyer simply decides they no longer want the item after receiving it in the condition described, the Seller is not obligated to accept a return.

A Seller's "No Returns" policy does NOT mean:
- The Buyer cannot open a dispute for a counterfeit, misrepresented, or non-functional item
- U-Shop cannot override the policy in a fraud or misrepresentation case
- The Seller's escrow funds are immune from refund in the event of a substantiated dispute
- The Buyer's Platform Minimum Buyer Protections are suspended

Any Seller who represents to a Buyer — in their store policy, in Platform messages, or verbally — that a "No Returns" policy prevents the Buyer from opening a U-Shop dispute is making a false representation. Such conduct constitutes a violation of the Seller Agreement and may result in account termination.

## 3. Qualifying Grounds for a Buyer Dispute

A Buyer may open a formal dispute on U-Shop on the following grounds:

| Ground | Description | Evidence Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Item Not Received (INR)** | The item was never delivered and the Seller cannot provide proof of dispatch or delivery | Buyer's written statement; lack of seller-provided tracking or meetup confirmation |
| **Not as Described (NAD)** | The item received is materially different from the listing — different model, different brand, different specifications | Photos/video of the received item with visible model identifier; comparison to listing photos |
| **Condition Misrepresentation** | The item's condition is significantly worse than the grade selected by the Seller | Photos/video clearly showing the defect; battery health screenshot where applicable |
| **Dead on Arrival (DOA)** | The item does not power on or is completely non-functional, with no defect disclosed in the listing | Video of the device failing to power on; confirmed serial number match to listing |
| **Counterfeit or Stolen** | The item is a replica, not the genuine product described, or is confirmed stolen | IMEI/serial number report; network blacklist check result; official verification from manufacturer or Ghana Police |
| **Wrong Item Sent** | The Seller sent a completely different product | Photos of received item with identifiers visible |

**Disputes opened on the following grounds are outside the scope of this Policy and are not U-Shop's responsibility:**
- Buyer's change of mind after receiving an accurately described, functional item
- Disputes about delivery timing where the item was ultimately received in the described condition
- Disagreements about the fair market value of the item
- Complaints about the Seller's communication style, where no transaction issue exists

### 4.1 The Manual Confirmation Path

If the Buyer confirms delivery before Day 7, the escrow release is immediate:

1. Buyer clicks "Confirm Delivery" in the Platform
2. A confirmation dialog warns: **"This action is irreversible. Once you confirm, payment will be released to the Seller immediately."**
3. Buyer confirms
4. U-Shop initiates payout to Seller's wallet (minus platform fee)
5. **Buyer has 48 hours from this confirmation** to open a dispute if an issue is subsequently discovered (e.g., hidden defect discovered upon deeper inspection)

### 4.2 The 48-Hour Post-Confirmation Window

The 48-hour dispute window after manual confirmation exists for situations where a defect was not immediately apparent upon initial inspection — for example, a battery issue that only manifests after a full charge cycle, or a camera defect only discovered when the feature is first used.

**This window is firm.** After 48 hours following Buyer confirmation (or after auto-release on Day 7), U-Shop will not accept a new dispute related to the same transaction unless there is clear evidence of deliberate fraud by the Seller (e.g., a confirmed counterfeit device or confirmed stolen IMEI that the Buyer could not reasonably have detected earlier).

## 5. The Dispute Process — Step by Step

### Step 1: Buyer Opens a Dispute

- Buyer navigates to the relevant order and clicks "Open Dispute"
- Buyer selects a dispute category (see Section 3) and provides a written description of the issue (minimum 20 characters)
- Buyer uploads photographic or video evidence (up to 5 files; video is strongly recommended for DOA claims)
- Dispute is registered and **escrow is immediately frozen** — the auto-release timer is suspended regardless of where the order sits in the timeline

**SLA:** Dispute system acknowledges receipt within 1 hour (automated).

### Step 2: Seller Notified and Given Right of Response

U-Shop notifies the Seller that a dispute has been opened. The Seller has **24 hours** to respond through the Platform with their account of the transaction and any supporting evidence (dispatch proof, condition photographs, delivery confirmation, in-app message records).

Failure to respond within 24 hours is not automatically a ruling against the Seller, but U-Shop may decide a case on available evidence if a Seller is non-responsive.

### Step 3: Trust & Safety Review

U-Shop's Trust & Safety team reviews all submitted evidence, the listing details (including photographs and condition grade selected), the in-app message thread, the transaction record, and the policy snapshot recorded at the time of purchase.

**Target review SLA:** First substantive response within 24 hours of dispute opening; final decision within 72 hours of dispute opening for standard cases. Complex cases involving external verification (IMEI checks, authentication requests) may take up to 5 business days.

During the review period, all parties are expected to cooperate. U-Shop may request additional evidence from either party. Failing to provide requested evidence within 24 hours may result in a decision based on available information.

### Step 4: U-Shop Issues a Decision

U-Shop's Trust & Safety team will issue a decision in one of the following forms:

| Decision | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Resolved in Buyer's Favour — Full Refund** | 100% of escrowed held amount refunded to Buyer via original payment method or Platform wallet credit. Seller receives nothing for this transaction. Platform fee is not refunded. |
| **Resolved in Buyer's Favour — Partial Refund** | A portion of the escrowed amount is refunded to the Buyer (e.g., where item was partially as described but with an undisclosed minor defect). Balance released to Seller. |
| **Resolved in Seller's Favour** | Escrow released to Seller's wallet in full (minus platform fee). No refund issued to Buyer. |
| **Mutually Agreed Resolution** | Parties have agreed a resolution; U-Shop implements the agreed outcome. |

### Step 5: Post-Decision

Both parties are notified of the decision and its rationale. The decision with respect to the escrowed funds is **final and binding** on both parties.

Either party may submit an appeal within **7 days** of receiving the decision if they can provide material new evidence that was not available during the original review. Appeals are reviewed by a senior Trust & Safety team member. A decision on appeal is final.

## 7. Abuse of the Dispute Process

### 7.1 Buyer Abuse

Using the dispute process dishonestly is fraud. Buyers who are found to have submitted false or fabricated dispute claims — for example, claiming non-delivery while retaining the item, or submitting manipulated photographs — will face:
- Immediate account suspension pending investigation
- Reversal of any refund issued on the basis of fraudulent evidence
- Permanent account termination upon confirmation of abuse
- Reporting to Ghana Police Service where the fraudulent conduct constitutes a criminal offence

### 7.2 Pattern of Abuse

Accounts with a dispute-to-transaction ratio that is disproportionately high will be flagged for Trust & Safety review. A pattern of disputes, even where individual disputes appear legitimate, may trigger a review of the account.

## 8. Seller Obligations in Disputes

By selling on U-Shop, you accept that:
- Disputes are an inherent part of marketplace commerce and do not, by themselves, constitute an allegation of intentional wrongdoing against you
- Your obligation to cooperate with U-Shop's dispute process is absolute — non-cooperation does not protect you; it only reduces the evidence available to support your position
- A dispute resolved in the Buyer's favour does not entitle you to seek reimbursement from U-Shop — your recourse is against the Buyer directly through appropriate legal channels if you believe the dispute was fraudulent
- Publicly disparaging U-Shop's dispute decisions, or attempting to intimidate Buyers who have opened disputes, constitutes a violation of the Seller Agreement
