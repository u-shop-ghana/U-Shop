## 1. Becoming a Seller on U-Shop

### 1.1 Eligibility

To operate as a Seller on U-Shop, you must:
- Be at least 18 years of age, or at least 16 years of age with a parent or legal guardian who accepts this Agreement on your behalf and accepts joint responsibility for your storefront
- Be a natural person, a registered business in Ghana, or a university student operating a personal storefront
- Provide accurate, complete, and verifiable identity information as required by our onboarding process
- Have a valid Mobile Money account or Ghana bank account registered in your name to receive payouts
- Not be subject to any court order, regulatory restriction, or prior Platform ban that would prevent your participation

### 1.2 Account Types and Fee Rates

Your platform commission rate is determined by your account type at the time of each sale:

| Account Type | Eligibility | Commission Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Verified Student Seller** | Successfully completed student verification with a recognised `.edu.gh` email or approved student ID | **5% per completed transaction** |
| **Standard / Reseller** | Any Seller who does not qualify for or has not completed student verification | **8% per completed transaction** |

You are responsible for maintaining the accuracy of your account type. If U-Shop discovers that a Seller obtained the Verified Student rate through misrepresentation, we reserve the right to retroactively charge the difference for all applicable past transactions and permanently revoke Verified Student status.

### 1.3 Your Store URL

Upon activation, you are assigned a unique storefront URL in the format `u-shop.com/store/[your-handle]`. Your handle:
- Must comply with our naming guidelines (no impersonation, no reserved terms, no offensive content)
- May be changed once after initial creation, after which it is permanently locked
- Remains the property of U-Shop. Upon account termination, your handle may be released or retired at U-Shop's discretion.
- Where a previous handle is changed, U-Shop will maintain a redirect from the old URL to the new URL for a period of 90 days

## 2. Listing Requirements

### 2.1 Mandatory Listing Standards

Every product listing on your storefront must:
- Accurately describe the product — brand, model, specifications, and actual condition
- Use only genuine photographs of the specific item being sold (not stock images or images of a similar item)
- Correctly select the applicable condition grade from U-Shop's six-tier system (New / Like New / Used — Excellent / Used — Good / Used — Fair / For Parts)
- State the full asking price in Ghana Cedis (GH₵)
- Disclose the available stock quantity

### 2.2 Condition Grade Definitions

By selecting a condition grade, you make a representation to Buyers that the item meets the criteria for that grade as defined and illustrated on the Platform. Misrepresenting condition grade is a material breach of this Agreement and constitutes grounds for immediate account suspension and refund of the Buyer at your expense.

### 2.3 IMEI and Serial Number Requirements

- **All phone listings** must include a valid IMEI number. The IMEI must belong to the actual device being sold and must not appear on any stolen device register accessible to U-Shop at the time of listing.
- **Laptop listings priced at GH₵ 500 or above** must include the device serial number.
- Providing a false or mismatched IMEI or serial number is fraud. U-Shop will report confirmed cases to the Ghana Police Service.

### 2.4 Prohibited Listings

You may not list:
- Stolen, counterfeit, or fraudulently obtained devices
- Devices with removed, tampered, or obscured serial numbers or IMEI numbers
- Items you do not have the legal right to sell
- Non-technology goods of any category
- Items prohibited under Ghanaian law, including items subject to import restrictions
- Goods that infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party

## 3. Escrow Terms — The Core of Your Financial Relationship with U-Shop

This section governs how your sale proceeds are held and released. Read it carefully. By accepting orders on U-Shop, you agree irrevocably to these terms.

### 3.1 How Escrow Works

When a Buyer purchases from your store:

1. **Payment Collection:** The Buyer pays the full purchase price (100% of the listed price) directly to U-Shop via our payment partner, Paystack. Paystack transfers the funds to U-Shop's holding account. At this point, the funds are held entirely by U-Shop.

2. **Escrow Hold Period:** U-Shop holds 100% of the funds received. The Seller receives nothing during this period. The funds are tracked in your seller dashboard as "Pending" or "In Escrow."

3. **Release Trigger — Buyer Confirmation:** Funds are released when the Buyer confirms receipt of the item through the Platform by clicking "Confirm Delivery." Upon Buyer confirmation:
   - U-Shop deducts the applicable platform commission fee
   - The remaining balance (your "Seller Proceeds") is credited to your Platform wallet
   - You may then request a payout to your registered MoMo or bank account

4. **Release Trigger — Automatic Release:** If the Buyer does not confirm delivery and does not open a dispute within the window specified in our Refund Policy (currently **7 days** from the date of confirmed delivery, or from the date the order was marked as shipped/handed over where delivery confirmation is not available), funds are automatically released to your wallet.

5. **Release Trigger — Dispute Resolution:** Where a Buyer opens a dispute, funds remain frozen in escrow until the dispute is resolved by U-Shop's Trust & Safety team. The release or refund of funds in a disputed transaction is governed by Section 3.4 of this Agreement.

### 3.2 U-Shop's Authority Over Escrow Funds

**You expressly acknowledge and agree that U-Shop has sole and final authority over the disposition of escrowed funds**, subject to the rules in this Agreement and our Refund Policy. Specifically:

- **U-Shop may refund the Buyer** from escrow where U-Shop's Trust & Safety team determines, in its reasonable discretion, that:
  - The item received by the Buyer is materially different from the listing description
  - The item received is defective, non-functional, counterfeit, or stolen
  - The Seller failed to dispatch the item within the required timeframe
  - The Seller engaged in fraudulent, deceptive, or abusive conduct in connection with the transaction
  - The evidence supports a conclusion that the Buyer's dispute claim is substantiated

- **U-Shop may release funds to the Seller** where U-Shop's Trust & Safety team determines that the Buyer's dispute is unsubstantiated, that the item was accurately described, or that the auto-release conditions have been met.

- **U-Shop's determination in any dispute is final** with respect to the escrowed funds. This does not prevent either party from pursuing separate legal remedies in relation to the underlying transaction, but the release or retention of funds in escrow is resolved definitively by U-Shop's decision.

- **You may not instruct, pressure, or attempt to circumvent** U-Shop's escrow determination. Any attempt to do so constitutes a material breach of this Agreement.

### 3.3 Platform Fee Deduction

The platform commission fee is deducted from the escrowed amount before your Seller Proceeds are calculated and credited to your wallet. You will never receive the platform fee — it is retained by U-Shop.

**Example calculation (8% commission, GH₵ 1,200 sale):**
- Buyer pays: GH₵ 1,200.00
- Platform commission (8%): GH₵ 96.00
- Seller Proceeds credited to wallet: GH₵ 1,104.00

**Example calculation (5% commission, GH₵ 1,200 sale — Verified Student):**
- Buyer pays: GH₵ 1,200.00
- Platform commission (5%): GH₵ 60.00
- Seller Proceeds credited to wallet: GH₵ 1,140.00

**The platform fee is non-refundable** once the transaction is processed, even where a refund is subsequently issued to the Buyer. This reflects the cost of providing the marketplace, payment processing, and dispute resolution services for the transaction.

### 3.4 Refunds and Disputes: Your Exposure

Where a dispute is resolved in the Buyer's favour, the refund is issued from the escrowed funds — meaning the Buyer receives back what they paid and **you receive nothing** for that transaction. U-Shop does not extend credit to cover refunds from funds you have already received.

You acknowledge that you remain financially exposed for a refund if:
- The item you sent does not match your listing description
- The item arrives damaged due to poor packaging (where you arranged delivery)
- A verified dispute establishes that the item is defective, counterfeit, or stolen
- You fail to dispatch the item within the required timeframe and the Buyer cancels

This financial exposure is the direct consequence of your control over and responsibility for the goods you list and sell.

### 3.5 Wallet and Payouts

- **Minimum payout:** GH₵ 20.00
- **Payout methods:** Mobile Money (MTN MoMo, Vodafone Cash, AirtelTigo Money) or linked bank account, via Paystack
- **Payout timing:** Payouts are initiated within 1–3 business days of a withdrawal request, subject to Paystack's transfer timelines and any applicable regulatory holds
- **Payout account changes:** Changes to your registered payout account must be verified through the account security process before taking effect. We will not process payouts to accounts added within 24 hours of a payout request as a fraud prevention measure.
- **Frozen wallets:** Your wallet balance may be frozen pending investigation of suspected fraud, an unresolved dispute, or a request from a competent authority. We will notify you of any such freeze as soon as we are legally permitted to do so.

## 4. Your Custom Return and Warranty Policy

### 4.1 Your Right to Set Your Own Policy

U-Shop's Platform allows you to configure a custom return and warranty policy for your storefront using our structured policy builder. You may set parameters including:
- Return window (No Returns / 24 hours / 3 days / 7 days / 14 days / 30 days)
- Warranty period and coverage scope (None / Dead on Arrival / Hardware Defects / Specified Period)
- Refund method preference (wallet credit / original payment method)

### 4.2 Your Legal Responsibility for Your Policy

**You are legally responsible for honouring the policy you set.** When a Buyer purchases from your store, the return and warranty policy terms active at the time of purchase are presented to the Buyer and become part of the contract between you and the Buyer. You cannot retrospectively change or withdraw a warranty or return commitment made in your policy at the time a Buyer completed a purchase.

A snapshot of your policy at the time of each transaction is recorded permanently in our system and constitutes the agreed terms between you and the Buyer for that specific transaction.

### 4.3 Platform Policy Supersedes Seller Policy — Critical

**U-Shop's Platform Minimum Buyer Protections supersede your custom policy in all circumstances where they conflict.** Specifically:

- **A "No Returns" policy does not prevent a Buyer from opening a fraud or misrepresentation dispute.** "No Returns" governs voluntary, change-of-mind returns only. It cannot be used to prevent a Buyer from disputing a counterfeit product, a device that arrived dead on arrival, or an item materially different from its description.
- **U-Shop's dispute resolution authority cannot be contractually excluded** by your store policy. A statement in your store policy such as "No disputes accepted" or "All sales final — no escrow intervention" has no legal effect within the Platform.
- **Buyers retain the right to dispute within U-Shop's specified window** regardless of your stated warranty period.

You understand and accept this hierarchy at the point of activating your storefront. If you do not accept U-Shop's authority to override your store policy in anti-fraud and misrepresentation cases, you may not operate a storefront on the Platform.

## 5. Order Fulfilment Obligations

### 5.1 Dispatch Timeframe

You must dispatch or arrange handover of an item within **48 hours** of receiving a confirmed, paid order (excluding weekends and public holidays in Ghana). If you are unable to fulfil an order within this timeframe, you must contact the Buyer immediately through the Platform's messaging system and agree a revised arrangement.

### 5.2 Failure to Fulfil

Where you fail to fulfil an order within a reasonable period, or where you notify U-Shop that you are unable to fulfil an order:
- U-Shop may cancel the order and issue a full refund to the Buyer from escrow
- Your platform reliability score will be negatively affected
- Repeated failure to fulfil constitutes a material breach of this Agreement

### 5.3 Description of Delivery Method

All transactions on U-Shop are campus- and person-to-person-oriented. You are responsible for:
- Clearly communicating your available delivery methods (in-person meetup, courier, etc.) to the Buyer
- Conducting in-person meetups in safe, public locations (university campuses, public buildings, bank premises)
- Providing tracking information or meetup confirmation details through the Platform's messaging system
